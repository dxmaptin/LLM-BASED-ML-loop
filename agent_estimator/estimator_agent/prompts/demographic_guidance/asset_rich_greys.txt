DEMOGRAPHIC: Asset Rich Greys
AGE: 60+, retired or near retirement
INCOME: High, significant assets, comfortable retirement
FINANCIAL: Wealthy elderly, financially secure

BEHAVIORAL CALIBRATION GUIDANCE:

Digital Behavior:
- MODERATE-HIGH digital banking adoption for elderly segment (75-80% range)
- Higher than average elderly due to affluence and education
- Still prefer traditional methods but more open to digital than typical elderly

Life Satisfaction:
- VERY HIGH life satisfaction (68-70% range)
- Combination of financial security and life stage acceptance
- Among highest satisfaction of all segments

Environmental Attitudes:
- MODERATE recycling behavior (45-55% range)
- MODERATE climate concern (20-25% range)
- VERY LOW environmentalist self-identification (5-8% range)
- VERY LOW concern about where products are made (10-12% range)
- Age-related lower priority despite resources
- Actions are pragmatic, not ideologically driven

Social/Ethical Consumption:
- MODERATE support for brands with social/environmental commitment (30-35% range)
- MODERATE local business support (15-18% range)
- Quality and reputation focused, not primarily values-driven

Financial Attitudes:
- VERY HIGH saving preference (79-82% "hate to borrow")
- MODERATE trust in comparison sites (37-40% - less necessity-driven than budget elderly)
- Financial conservatism despite wealth

Shopping Behavior:
- Quality-focused rather than values-driven
- Brand loyalty higher due to age
- Can afford ethical choices but not strongly motivated by environmental values

KEY INSIGHT: Affluent elderly show high satisfaction and good digital adoption, but environmental IDENTITY remains very low despite moderate environmental ACTIONS (like recycling). They support ethical brands moderately but do NOT identify as environmentalists.
