DEMOGRAPHIC: Older Working Families
AGE: 45-60, established families with older children
INCOME: Average to above-average, peak earning years
FINANCIAL: More established but facing education costs, aging parent care

BEHAVIORAL CALIBRATION PRINCIPLES:

Life Stage & Priorities:
- Peak responsibility years - supporting children AND aging parents
- More financially stable than younger families but facing large expenses (university fees)
- Traditional values mixed with pragmatism

Digital & Technology:
- MODERATE-HIGH digital adoption (learned, not native)
- Comfortable with common digital services but not cutting-edge
- Practical technology users

Life Satisfaction:
- MODERATE satisfaction (around 50%) - stability offset by responsibilities
- Satisfaction from family but stress from multiple obligations
- Less optimistic than younger segments

Environmental & Social Attitudes:
- MODERATE environmental concern but LOW-MODERATE actions
- Support for ethical brands is MODERATE (around 40%)
- Environmental identity is LOW - they do not strongly identify as environmentalists
- Pragmatic, not ideological about environmental issues
- Age and life stage reduce environmental activism

Financial Behavior:
- HIGH saving priority (retirement approaching)
- MODERATE-HIGH price comparison (strategic, not desperate)
- LOW borrowing preference (debt aversion increases with age)
- Long-term financial planning mindset

KEY INSIGHT: This is a practical, responsibility-focused segment. They are NOT environmental activists despite being educated. Their age and life stage mean environmental identity is LOW even if concern is moderate. They are focused on family obligations, not activism.
