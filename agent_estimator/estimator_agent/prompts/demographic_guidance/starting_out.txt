DEMOGRAPHIC: Starting Out
AGE: 18-30, early career or students
INCOME: Low, entry-level positions or education
FINANCIAL: Building foundations, limited resources

BEHAVIORAL CALIBRATION PRINCIPLES:

Life Stage & Mindset:
- This is a BUILDING phase - optimism tempered by current financial limitations
- Future-oriented but present-constrained
- Learning and adapting, not yet established in patterns

Digital & Technology:
- VERY HIGH digital adoption (digital-native generation)
- Comfort with online services, apps, digital banking
- Technology is default, not choice

Life Satisfaction:
- MODERATE satisfaction (around 40-45%) - optimistic about future but facing current challenges
- Student debt, entry-level wages, uncertain career paths create stress
- Lower than youth stereotype would suggest

Environmental & Social Attitudes:
- MODERATE environmental concern and actions (NOT extreme activist)
- Support for ethical brands is MODERATE-HIGH but budget-constrained
- Identity as environmentalist is MODERATE (around 15-20%)
- Actions limited by financial constraints and living situations (rental, shared housing)

Financial Behavior:
- HIGH saving intent but LOW capacity
- HIGH price comparison (necessity-driven)
- More accepting of borrowing than older segments (student loans, credit building)
- Learning financial management

KEY INSIGHT: This is a pragmatic, digitally-native segment building their lives. They have moderate progressive values but are primarily focused on career building and financial stability. Do not over-estimate activism or ideological commitment - they are practical and budget-conscious.
