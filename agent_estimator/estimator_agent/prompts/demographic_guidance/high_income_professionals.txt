DEMOGRAPHIC: High Income Professionals
AGE: 30-55, career-established professionals
INCOME: Top 20%, high-earning careers
FINANCIAL: Financially comfortable, high disposable income

BEHAVIORAL CALIBRATION GUIDANCE:

Digital Behavior:
- VERY HIGH digital banking adoption (88-92% range)
- High technology adoption across all domains
- Time-conscious, convenience-driven behaviors

Life Satisfaction:
- HIGH satisfaction from career success and income (59-62% range)
- Work-life balance stress present but HIGH overall satisfaction
- Generally positive outlook and future orientation

Environmental Attitudes:
- LOW climate concern (23-26% range) - NOT as high as income would suggest
- MODERATE recycling behavior (40-44% range)
- LOW environmentalist self-identification (7-10% range)
- VERY LOW concern about where products are made (8-11% range)
- Can afford ethical choices but NOT strongly motivated
- Actions are convenience-driven and time-efficient, not ideologically driven

Social/Ethical Consumption:
- HIGH support for brands with social/environmental commitment (45-50% range)
- MODERATE local business support (12-15% range)
- IMPORTANT: High brand support (47%) is driven by QUALITY and REPUTATION, not environmental ideology
- Quality-conscious purchasing, willing to pay for premium brands

Financial Attitudes:
- MODERATE-HIGH saving preference (71-74% "hate to borrow")
- HIGH trust in comparison sites despite income (56-59% - efficiency-seeking, not necessity)
- Strategic, sophisticated money management

Shopping Behavior:
- Quality and brand reputation primary drivers
- Moderate-high brand loyalty based on quality
- Time-efficient purchasing patterns
- Premium brand preference

KEY INSIGHT: This group has HIGH life satisfaction (60%) and HIGH ethical brand support (47%) but LOW environmental behaviors and ideology. They support ethical brands for QUALITY and REPUTATION reasons, not environmental commitment. Do NOT over-estimate their environmental actions - climate concern is LOW (24%), recycling is only MODERATE (42%). They are convenience and quality-driven, NOT environmentally driven.
